Helping researchers get a buy in for their research findings

Looppanel had a highlights view for all the notes tagged in research calls. Since we didn't have the project structure at the time, all the highlights were shown in a flat list.

Although this gave visibility across calls, but it was hard to do anything out of it.

Since projects were introduced in Looppanel, which in turn had questions scripts attached to each, we had an opportunity to redesign how the highlights looked like and make it easier for researchers to make sense of their data.

We looked at the existing tools of researchers, where their data stayed, and how they helped them take better decisions.

We looked at Figjam, Miro, Dovetail etc. & realised they zoomed in and out of their data regularly to make sense of their data.

Insights
1. First, they would tag notes at an individual call level, then they would zoom out to see all the notes together, then zoom in to see the details of a particular tag.
2. It was also easier for them to share their findings with stakeholders since their entire organization was using those tools.
3. Getting a buy in for research got easier if the summary was evidence backed, e.g. if a stakeholder asked why do we need to build this feature, the researcher could show them the exact notes and highlights that led to this conclusion.

Since we were working with a small team, it was hard to create create a sticky note canvas due to tech constraints and risk.

So I explored a bunch of options where we could show data across calls for a project, and also allow researchers to zoom in and out of their data.

We explored a few directions
1. First, showing data by participant name at the top, and then the highlights for each participant below.
2. Then, showing data by question script at the top, and then the highlights for each question script below.
3. Show a side-by-side view of the question script and the highlights for each question.

Finally we landed on creating collapsible sections for each question in the script with the highlights for each question below.

We also added a summary view that allowed researchers to share their findings with stakeholders.

First, they'd select multiple notes under each question, and then tag those to an insight.
