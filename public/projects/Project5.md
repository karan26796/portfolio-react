### 🎬 Call/Editor screen redesign
#### In the older call/editor screen, there were 3 types of views to show text linked to the transcript.

- A **video snippet** created from selecting the transcript text and adding a tag.
- A **moment** bookmarked during note-taking.
- A **note** taken during note-taking.

Due to multiple views of the same transcript text, the screen looked inconsistent. Users were also confused with the purpose of each. The addition of a **question script** would've added more complexity.

#### 💅 Updates to the Call/Editor screen
**Overall uniform view** — Cleaner view to add tags, see the timestamp, etc.

### 📝 AI-powered Notetaker
#### A 1-hour interview was consuming 5–6 hours of tagging and summarization.
To drive **research velocity**, we designed an AI-powered note-taker that lets researchers capture **structured insights in real-time** during Zoom calls — linked directly to the transcript.

Early iterations looked like a chat window, which felt too transactional. We pivoted to a **document-centric layout** — familiar, like Notion or Google Docs — and integrated **question scripts** directly into the note-taking flow so researchers could stay focused without losing structure.

</section>
